# Sentence Reading Efficiency Score Report

## Introduction to ROAR - Sentence Reading Efficiency

***ROAR - Sentence Reading Efficiency*** examines the ability to **silent reading fluency and comprehension for individual sentences**. Students need both accuracy in decoding and fluency at the sentence level in order to achieve fluency in passage reading. Research shows that low fluency is a barrier to comprehension for many students. Students not receiving support for their foundational reading skills may struggle to catch up in overall reading ability. This assessment is well-designed for screening students who may be struggling with reading comprehension in part because of inaccurate decoding of words and/or slow and effortful reading.

**This score report** provides information about the distribution of sentence-reading efficiency scores across each grade, as well as how each student’s skills compare to the national norms for their age group.

**The assessment** provides a measure of reading fluency. During the test, a sentence is displayed on the screen, and students categorize the sentence as true or false. e.g., *“Cats live in the ocean”* is false, and, *“Some kids like ice cream”* is true.

## Interpreting the Results
The Test of Silent Reading Efficiency and Comprehension (TOSREC) is a standardized paper-based test that was
normed on a nationally representative sample, and [has research support for its use as a screening instrument](https://doi.org/10.1177/1534508411395556). Stanford is currently working to validate ROAR - Sentence Reading
Efficiency for its ability to predict TOSREC scores; as such, the TOSREC equivalent scores in this report are based
upon the data we have collected so far. ROAR - Sentence Reading Efficiency provides a highly efficient way to
screen a group of students for acute literacy needs and strengths without expending the time and resources to
individually administer lengthy assessments.

The vertical cut scores represent TOSREC thresholds for classifying students into different risk categories: *Above Average*, *Average*, *Below Average*, *Low*, or *Very Low*.

- **Above Average**: Students whose sentence reading efficiency is above the 76th percentile.
- **Average**: Students whose sentence reading efficiency is between the 75th and 24th percentile.
- **Below Average**: Students whose sentence reading efficiency is between the 23rd and 9th percentile.
- **Low**: Students whose sentence reading efficiency is between the 8th and 3rd percentile.
- **Very Low**: Students whose sentence reading efficiency is below the 2nd percentile.

### Notes on Interpreting this Score Report

- Results from this rapid screener should be interpreted in the context of other information you have about each student’s reading ability. For example, a student who is not fully engaged during the assessment may receive a score that does not reflect their true ability.
- Students who need extra support in sentence-reading efficiency may need more support with decoding skills or with the development of fluency.
- Scores should be interpreted in conjunction with other ROAR assessments:
    - A student who is flagged as needing support on ROAR Sentence Reading Efficiency but not on ROAR Single Word Reading likely needs more support in developing automaticity. The student is likely reading accurately at the word-level but reading too slowly to maintain understanding of a whole sentence.
    - A student who is flagged as needing support on ROAR Single Word Recognition likely has decoding issues that are impacting fluency, and would benefit from more support in decoding skills.
    - A student who is flagged as needing support on ROAR Phonological Awareness may have issues in phonological awareness that are impacting the development of both word decoding and fluency. The student would likely benefit from support in phonological awareness.
- Ongoing research continues to refine thresholds for identifying students who may benefit from additional support. Defining thresholds for identifying students who may benefit from additional support is part of our ongoing research.
